## Overview

Granular aggregates exhibit emergent structural behavior that is difficult to predict analytically but can be explored through physical simulation and robotic deposition experiments. This project develops a grammar for architectural space based on these principles.

## Research Questions

- How can the stochastic behavior of granular materials be used as a design resource rather than a constraint?
- What deposition strategies allow for spatial differentiation within a continuous aggregate?

## Methods

DEM (Discrete Element Method) simulations were used to model grain interactions. A robotic arm with a custom deposition end-effector was programmed to pour aggregates in controlled sequences, producing spatial enclosures of varying density.
