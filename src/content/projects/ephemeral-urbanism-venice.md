## Overview

A small but significant contribution to the pavilion *Ephemeral Urbanism*, curated by Rahul Mehrotra and Felipe Vera for the 2016 Venice Architecture Biennale. The pavilion questioned the dominant discourse around permanence in architecture, representing the approximately 700 million people worldwide who inhabit temporary settlements — from religious gatherings and disaster response camps to extraction sites and refugee landscapes.

The course at Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez, led by Felipe Vera and Jannett Sordi, focused on researching and mapping these ephemeral urban phenomena. Working from over 600 global case studies across seven thematic categories — religious, celebratory, transactional, extractive, military, refuge, and disaster — the course produced data-driven analysis of how temporary cities form, grow, and dissolve.

## Contribution

The research and data gathered during the course were used to project models onto maps — visualizing patterns of change and indicators of ephemeral settlement across different contexts. This work was subsequently incorporated as part of the projections shown within the Venice pavilion alongside contributions from other students and collaborators from Harvard GSD and UAI DesignLab.

## Thematic Framework

The pavilion organized its 600+ case studies into seven landscape categories:

- Religious ephemeral landscapes
- Celebration landscapes
- Transaction landscapes
- Extraction landscapes
- Military ephemeral landscapes
- Refuge landscapes
- Disaster response landscapes

## Role & Tools

- Urban research and data gathering — ephemeral city case studies
- Cartographic projection — data modeled onto maps to visualize change patterns and symptoms of ephemerality
- UAI DesignLab — institutional framework and course context
