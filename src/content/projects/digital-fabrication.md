## Overview

A five-week intensive workshop exploring the design space opened by digital fabrication technologies. Participants developed projects using laser cutting, 3-axis CNC routing, and basic robotic arm programming.

## Projects

Twelve projects were developed, ranging from acoustic panels derived from wave simulation to structural joinery systems informed by traditional woodworking. Each project required formal documentation of the computational-to-fabrication pipeline.

## Reflection

The workshop revealed a consistent tension between computational freedom and fabrication constraint—a productive friction that defines the discipline.
