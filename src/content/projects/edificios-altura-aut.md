## Overview

Developed in collaboration with MetaLab — the innovation and digital fabrication laboratory of the Universidad Autónoma de Temuco — this project created a prototype VR simulation environment focused on high-rise building design and seismic resilience.

The goal was to translate complex structural concepts — typically communicated through static documents and 2D drawings — into an immersive, navigable experience that students and professionals could explore in real time.

## Experience Design

The simulation was structured as a multi-room walkthrough with distinct stations, each covering a specific dimension of high-rise architecture:

- **Seismic response simulations** — interactive scenarios showing how different structural systems behave during earthquakes, including strategies for energy dissipation and lateral load management
- **Materials and regulations** — BIM-linked environments presenting material properties, load specifications, and Chilean building code requirements in context
- **Multiplayer local sessions** — multiple users sharing the same virtual space simultaneously, enabling guided tours and collaborative exploration
- **Cross-platform support** — Oculus Quest for fully immersive VR and PC version with an integrated chat system for hybrid sessions

## Role & Tools

- Unreal Engine 5 — environment, interaction systems, simulation logic
- Oculus Quest integration + PC build with chat
- BIM data integration for material and normative content
- MetaLab collaboration for hardware setup and user testing
