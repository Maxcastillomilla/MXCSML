## Overview

This project explores the creation of structures based on modular volumes and connection rules, forming a constantly growing 3D puzzle-like assembly system. As the structure evolves, the volumes are reinterpreted and transformed into tensioned shapes using wool, fabric, and resin, resulting in a flexible and organic configuration.

The research aims to develop new construction methodologies based on the interaction between geometry, materiality, and structural tension, enabling the adaptation and expansion of forms.

## Tools & Methods

- **Kangaroo Physics** (Grasshopper) — simulation of tensioned and flexible geometries
- **Wasp** (Grasshopper) — modular aggregation with connection rules
- **Biomorpher** — evolutionary form exploration
- Wool frame physical prototyping with resin casting

## Key Outcomes

- Modular volumetric grammar with simple and complex connection rules
- Physical prototypes combining rigid modules and tensioned fabric/wool surfaces
- Wasp composition diagrams showing growth logic and assembly sequences
