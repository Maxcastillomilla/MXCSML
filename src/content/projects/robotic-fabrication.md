## Overview

This project investigates the integration of robotic fabrication processes with parametric computational design workflows. The central research question concerns how automated fabrication systems can be adapted to produce formally complex architectural components that remain structurally performative.

## Methodology

The workflow begins with structural simulation outputs, which are translated into robot toolpath instructions via a custom Python library interfacing with ROS. Constraints from material behavior, robot kinematics, and safety envelopes are embedded as optimization parameters.

## Outcomes

Three prototypes were produced at 1:1 scale, demonstrating the feasibility of producing non-standard concrete elements with tolerances below 3mm. The workflow was subsequently documented and made available as an open-source toolkit.
