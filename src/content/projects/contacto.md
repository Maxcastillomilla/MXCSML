## Overview

Contacto is a thesis project that aims to make sexuality in people with disabilities more visible, particularly those with psychomotor disabilities and genital sensory loss. It focuses on raising awareness and developing solutions such as a sexuality manual and a line of adapted sex toys for paraplegic individuals.

Made with Ecoflex silicone and medical gel to enhance sensory experiences, these products draw inspiration from soft robot technology to improve design adaptability. The project collaborates with healthcare professionals in rehabilitation centers to support users' sexual reintegration.

My role within the team focused on parametric design and prototyping, contributing to the development of adaptable and functional solutions.

## Tools & Methods

- **Kangaroo Physics** (Grasshopper) — simulation of soft, deformable geometries
- **Biomorpher** (Grasshopper) — evolutionary form exploration across morphological variants
- **Kangaroo Simulation** — structural and material behavior testing
- Ecoflex silicone and medical gel casting for physical prototypes

## Key Outcomes

- Full product line of parametrically designed adaptive prototypes
- 360° render series documenting morphological variants
- Texture sampler for material sensory evaluation
- Collaboration framework with rehabilitation center professionals
