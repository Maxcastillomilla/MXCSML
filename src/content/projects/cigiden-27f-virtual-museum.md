## Overview

On February 27, 2010, Chile was struck by one of the most powerful earthquakes ever recorded — magnitude 8.8 — affecting six regions from Valparaíso to Araucanía, leaving 526 people dead, over 1.8 million affected, and causing economic losses equivalent to 12.5% of Chile's GDP. A decade later, CIGIDEN — Chile's national center for integrated natural disaster risk management, funded by ANID's FONDAP program — sought to commemorate the anniversary through public education.

With COVID-19 canceling all in-person exhibitions, the project became **¡Chile: Territorio en Movimiento!** — a downloadable virtual museum built in Unreal Engine combining scientific simulation with game-like mechanics, designed specifically for student audiences.

## Experience Design

The museum recreated computational simulations projected over scaled 3D terrain models of three Chilean locations: **Antofagasta, Valparaíso, and Villarrica** — each chosen for their exposure to distinct hazard types.

Visitors could explore and interact with:

- **Earthquake simulations** — ground motion propagation across regional terrain models
- **Tsunami scenarios** — wave dynamics and coastal inundation zones
- **Volcanic eruptions and lahars** — Villarrica hazard simulations with flow paths
- **Landslide (aluvión) events** — slope failure and debris flow visualizations
- **Avatars, videos and audio** — documentary content keeping the experience narrative-driven
- **Secret zone** — unlocked only after visiting all exhibition rooms, rewarding full exploration

## Role & Tools

- Unreal Engine — 3D environments, avatar systems, simulation layers, interaction logic
- Blueprint visual scripting — room transitions, unlock mechanics, media playback
- CIGIDEN scientific data for geographically accurate disaster simulations
- Distributed as a standalone Windows executable for schools and universities
